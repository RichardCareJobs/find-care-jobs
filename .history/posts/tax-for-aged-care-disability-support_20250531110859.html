Work-Related Tax Deductions for Aged Care and Disability Support Workers in Australia

Published 31 May 2025

If you work in aged care or disability support, you may be entitled to claim a range of tax deductions to help reduce your taxable income. Knowing what you can and can’t claim can make a big difference at tax time.

Common Deductible Expenses

  • Uniform and Laundry: Branded or compulsory uniforms and associated laundry costs are usually deductible.
  • Work-Related Travel: If you travel between clients, you can often claim vehicle expenses (or a cents-per-kilometre rate), public transport fares, and tolls.
  • Training and Education: Courses directly related to your job, including first aid or Certificate IV upgrades, are typically deductible if not reimbursed by your employer.
  • Union and Membership Fees: Professional association fees or union dues can also be claimed.
  • Work Equipment: Items such as a diary, work bag, PPE (like gloves or masks), or a phone used for work-related calls may be partially deductible.

Important Things to Remember

  • Keep detailed records and receipts for all deductions.
  • Home-to-work travel is generally not claimable, but travel between workplaces is.
  • If you’re unsure, consult a registered tax agent or refer to the ATO’s guides for aged care and support workers.

Tip: The ATO’s occupation-specific guides are a great resource and can help ensure you’re not missing out on legitimate claims.
