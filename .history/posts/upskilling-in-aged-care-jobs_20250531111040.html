Upskilling in Aged Care: How to Advance Your Career and Make a Bigger Impact

Published 28 May 2025

Australia’s ageing population is driving demand for skilled aged care workers. Upskilling is not only a smart move for career progression—it’s also essential for delivering high-quality care in an evolving sector. Whether you're aiming to take on more responsibility or specialise in a particular area, there are many ways to build your skills.

Popular Upskilling Pathways in Aged Care

  • Certificate IV in Ageing Support: This builds on a Certificate III and is often required for team leader or supervisory roles.
  • Medication Administration Courses: These short courses can enable you to safely administer medications under supervision.
  • Dementia and Palliative Care Training: With increasing demand for specialist care, training in these areas is highly valuable.
  • Leadership and Management Qualifications: If you're ready to step into a coordinator or facility manager role, consider a Diploma of Leadership and Management.

How to Get Started

Many TAFEs and RTOs offer flexible delivery modes, including part-time, online, and evening classes. If you’re working in aged care already, speak with your employer about training subsidies or internal development programs.

Did You Know?

Upskilling can make you eligible for higher-paying roles and increase your confidence when applying for new jobs. It’s also a great way to stay engaged and avoid burnout by bringing new meaning and challenge to your work.
