How to Transfer or Validate Your Overseas Qualifications to Work in Australia

Published: 12 June 2025

If you’ve recently moved to Australia or are planning to immigrate, you may be wondering whether your existing qualifications will be recognised here. The good news is that many overseas qualifications can be transferred, but it’s important to go through the correct validation process — especially if you want to work in regulated industries like aged care, disability support, nursing, or allied health.

In this article, we’ll guide you through how to validate your overseas qualifications, what agencies are involved, and where to get help.

Why Recognition Matters

Australia places a strong emphasis on professional standards. Depending on your field, your qualifications may need to be formally recognised before you can work in your role. In sectors like healthcare or social services, recognition isn’t just about job prospects — it’s often a legal requirement for employment.

Step 1: Identify Whether Your Job Is Regulated

Some roles in Australia are regulated occupations, meaning they require licensing or registration with a government body. Examples in the care sector include:

  • Nurses and midwives – regulated by the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia (NMBA)
  • Aged care or disability support workers – not always formally regulated, but many employers require certifications like a Certificate III in Individual Support
  • Allied health professionals – regulated by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)

If your occupation is regulated, you will likely need to undergo a skills assessment or register with the relevant authority.

Step 2: Have Your Qualifications Assessed

Australia uses a national framework to compare international qualifications:

Qualification assessments are handled by agencies such as VETASSESS or Trades Recognition Australia (TRA), depending on your field.

For care-related roles:

  • Vocational qualifications (e.g. diplomas in care or social services) may require assessment through VETASSESS.
  • Healthcare professionals like nurses or physiotherapists are typically assessed through AHPRA.

Use the Australian Government’s Skill Recognition Finder to check the appropriate assessing body for your occupation.

Step 3: Apply for Recognition or Enrol in Gap Training

If your qualification is fully recognised, you may be ready to work in your field. If it's partially recognised, you may need to complete:

  • Gap training through a Registered Training Organisation (RTO)
  • English language proficiency tests such as IELTS or OET
  • Supervised practice or placements

For aged care and disability roles, many migrants choose to enrol in a Certificate III or IV to meet local standards. Some providers offer Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL), which may shorten the course.

Step 4: Apply for Jobs and Start Building Local Experience

Once your qualifications are validated or supplemented, you can start applying for work. Many care providers value local experience, so don’t be discouraged if you need to start with an entry-level role — it’s a valuable stepping stone and often leads to long-term opportunities.
