How to Write a Cover Letter for Disability Support Work

Published 13 May 2025

Your cover letter is a chance to speak directly to an employer and show them why you're a great fit. In disability support, it's your opportunity to highlight your compassion, communication skills, and dedication to person-centred care.

1. Start Strong

Open with a clear statement of your interest in the role and where you saw the job advertised. Mention the organisation by name to show you've done your research.

2. Align with the Role

Explain how your skills and experience match what the employer is looking for. Use keywords from the job ad where appropriate.

3. Show Your Passion

Briefly explain why you enjoy working in disability support. A short story or example can help show your motivation and values.
